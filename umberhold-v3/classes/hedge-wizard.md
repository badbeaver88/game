# Hedge Wizard

## Class Summary

The **Hedge Wizard** is a mire-born full caster who blends raw elemental force, scavenged relic craft, and spiteful anti-magic into a volatile arcane style.

### Core Identity
- **Primary Attributes:** Intellect / Luck
- **Level 10 Title:** Black Archon
- **Hit Die:** d6
- **Armor:** No armor
- **Shield:** Cannot equip shield
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** Quarterstaff, dagger
- **Spellcasting:** Full caster using **Intellect**
- **Special Role:** Rear-rank blaster, utility caster, relic manipulator, anti-magic specialist

## Features

### Spellcasting
You can cast spells as a **full caster**.

Rules:
- the Hedge Wizard uses **Intellect** as its spellcasting attribute
- spell access and spell recovery follow Umberhold's current spellcasting rules
- at character creation, a Hedge Wizard gains all currently approved **HW cantrips** and **HW 1st-level spells**

---

### Blackwell Casting
When you cast a spell, there is a chance that the spell does **not** consume its spell slot.

### Formula
```text
5% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

---

### Uncapped Attribute Growth
You can increase:
- one attribute by **2**
- or two attributes by **1** each

---

### Charge Siphon
When you use an item with charges, there is a chance that the charge is **not** expended.

### Formula
```text
10% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

---

### Elemental Mastery
Choose one element to master:
- **Fire** (**Pyromancer**)
- **Cold** (**Cryomancer**)
- **Lightning** (**Stormcaller**)
- **Acid** (**Venomancer**)

Whenever you cast a spell that deals damage of your chosen type, it deals **an additional damage die**.

---

### Spell Mastery
Choose one **1st-level spell**.

You may cast that spell as a **cantrip**.
It no longer consumes a spell slot.

---

### Vampiric Conduit
When you kill a creature using damage of your chosen **Elemental Mastery** type, there is a chance to siphon its essence.

On a successful siphon:
- you heal for **10%** of the elemental damage dealt
- you cannot heal above your **maximum hit points**

### Formula
```text
35% + 3% per point of your Luck modifier
```

---

### Arcane Defiance
You have a chance to completely resist any spell that would affect you, even if you are **not** the direct target.

### Formula
```text
10% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

---

### Cataclysm Casting
Whenever you cast a **damaging spell**, roll an **unmodified d20**.

On a result of **20**:
- the spell becomes a **critical spell**
- the spell deals **double damage dice**

---

### Spell Reflection
When a spell **directly targets** you, there is a chance to reflect that spell back at the caster.

### Formula
```text
5% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

### Restriction
This does **not** apply to:
- area-of-effect spells
- abilities that do not specifically target you

---

## Notes

### Current Role
The Hedge Wizard is currently defined as:
- rear-rank full caster
- elemental damage specialist
- utility and control spellcaster
- charge-efficient relic user
- anti-magic survivability caster

### Current Spell Access
Under current Umberhold spellcasting canon, the Hedge Wizard begins with:
- all approved **HW cantrips**
- all approved **HW 1st-level spells**

The current early spell list already supports:
- **lightning** mastery through spells such as **Arcbolt**
- **acid** mastery through spells such as **Venomspit** and **Zarveth's Acid Darts**
- broad utility and control through spells such as **Beguile**, **Blind**, **Sleep**, **Identify**, **Feather Fall**, and **Witchlight**

**Fire** and **Cold** mastery options will become more fully supported as higher-level Hedge Wizard spells are authored.

### Current Open Mechanical Questions
These may need later clarification:
- how **Elemental Mastery** applies to multi-hit spells such as **Zarveth's Acid Darts**
- whether **Blackwell Casting**, **Charge Siphon**, **Arcane Defiance**, and **Spell Reflection** have explicit floors or caps with extreme Luck modifiers
- whether **Spell Reflection** works on any directly targeted spell, including save-based spells, or only on certain targeting structures
- whether **Vampiric Conduit** uses only the killing hit's elemental damage or a wider damage total from area spells
- how **Cataclysm Casting** interacts with spells that already gain extra dice from **Elemental Mastery**
