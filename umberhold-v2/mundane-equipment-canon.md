# Umberhold Mundane Equipment Canon

This document captures the current approved rules for:
- mundane settlement gear
- free class starting equipment
- hurled combat items
- special rules for specific mundane items

It does **not** modify `MASTER.md`.

---

## 1. General Rules

Mundane items are commonly available in settlements.

### Shop Categories
- **Equipment Shops** stock general adventuring goods in addition to weapons and armor.
- **Temples** stock items of a religious nature.

### Measurement and Tracking
- item weight is measured in **stones**
- costs below use current Umberhold coin names, with legacy D&D-style abbreviations preserved in parentheses for reference
- food is **not** tracked
- arrows are **not** tracked

## 5. Hurled Combat Items

Certain mundane items may be used as hurled weapons in combat.

### Hurled Attack Rule
When a hurled mundane item requires an attack roll:
- roll **`d20 + Agility bonus`**
- compare the result against the target's **AC**
- the attack has **range 3**

The item's listed on-hit effect then resolves normally.

---

## 6. Mundane Item Combat Effects

### Holy Water
- **range 3**
- deals **`2d6` holy damage**
- affects **undead only**

### Flask of Oil
- **range 3**
- on hit: deals **`2d4` fire damage**
- on hit: applies **Burning**
- at the start of the target's next turn: deals **`1d4` fire damage**

## 7. Free Starting Equipment

At character creation, the following classes begin with free mundane gear:

- **Friar** — **Holy Symbol of Solace**
- **Shadowman** — **Thieves' Tools**
- **Troubadour** — **one instrument of choice** from the approved mundane instrument list

---

## 8. Special Item Rules

### Magnifying Glass
- grants **`+2` to detect traps** while in inventory
- does **not** need to be equipped

## 9. Approved Rules Captured Here

This document locks in the following:
- mundane settlement stock is divided between equipment shops and temples
- weights for these items use stones
- food and arrows are not tracked
- torch, hooded lantern, flask of oil, thieves' tools, and magnifying glass are standard equipment-shop stock
- holy water and holy symbols of Solace are standard temple stock
- Troubadours start with one mundane instrument of choice
- Friars start with a holy symbol of Solace
- Shadowmen start with thieves' tools
- hurled mundane combat items use **`d20 + Agility bonus vs AC`**
- hurled mundane combat items have **range 3** unless a specific item says otherwise
- holy water deals **`2d6` holy damage** to undead only
- flasks of oil deal **`2d4` fire damage** on hit and apply **Burning** for **`1d4` fire damage** at the start of the target's next turn
- magnifying glasses grant **`+2` to detect traps** while carried and do not require equipping
