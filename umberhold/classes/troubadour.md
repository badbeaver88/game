# Troubadour

## Class Summary

The **Troubadour** is a silver-tongued support caster who shapes battle through song, bolstering allies, unraveling enemies, and sustaining the party through performance and presence.

### Core Identity
- **Primary Attributes:** Personality / Luck
- **Level 10 Title:** Maestro of Ruin
- **Hit Die:** d8
- **Armor:** Light armor
- **Shield:** Cannot equip shield
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** All single-handed melee weapons
- **Spellcasting:** Half caster using **Personality**
- **Special Role:** Rear-rank support caster, song-based buffer/debuffer, morale manipulator, party sustain specialist

### Starting Gear
- begins play with **one mundane instrument of choice**:
  - **Flute**
  - **Lute**
  - **Horn**
  - **Drums**
  - **Pipes**

## Features

### Spellcasting
You can cast spells as a **half caster**.

Rules:
- the Troubadour uses **Personality** as its spellcasting attribute
- spell access and spell recovery follow Umberhold's current spellcasting rules
- at character creation, a Troubadour gains all currently approved **T cantrips** and **T 1st-level spells**

Current approved early spell access:
- **Cantrips:** Mocking Words
- **1st-level spells:** Witchlight, Zarveth's Acid Darts, Zarveth's Floating Candle

---

### Instrumentalist
You may use instruments that you find, including those of **magical** nature.

---

### Gift of Song
You can perform **Songs** to support your party in combat.

Rules:
- starting or maintaining a Song requires a **bonus action**
- Songs do **not** require an instrument
- a Song lasts for the duration of combat
- a Song ends if you are affected by a **Silence** effect
- a Song ends if you cast a spell
- you may still **attack** while singing
- you may perform a number of Songs equal to **3 + your level** before needing to **Rest**
- Resting restores all Song uses
- at character creation, choose **one Song** from the Song list
- the Troubadour does **not** benefit from their own Songs

---

### Silver Performance
Your party may stay at inns for **free**, as you perform in exchange for food and lodging.

Requirement:
- you must have an **instrument** in your inventory, whether mundane or magical

---

### Beguiling Resistance
You have **resistance** to spells and effects that attempt to:
- charm you
- beguile you
- or otherwise manipulate your mind

---

### Songcraft
You learn **one additional Song** from the Song list.

---

### Uncapped Attribute Growth
You can increase:
- one attribute by **2**
- or two attributes by **1** each

---

### Unshaken Chorus
While you are performing a Song, you are **immune** to the **Frightened** condition.

---

### Voice Unbroken
Your voice cannot be silenced by **magical or mundane means**.

---

### Endless Aria
You can now cast spells **without ending your Song**.

---

### Arcane Refrain
While performing a Song, there is a chance that any spell you cast does **not** consume a spell slot.

### Formula
```text
5% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

## Troubadour Songs

| Song Name | Effect |
|---|---|
| **March of Iron Vows** | All allies gain **+1 AC**. There is a **1% chance per point of your Luck modifier** that this bonus becomes **+2 AC**. |
| **Hymn of the Unbroken** | All allies gain **+1 to all saving throws**. There is a **1% chance per point of your Luck modifier** that this bonus becomes **+2 to all saving throws**. |
| **Warcaller's Crescendo** | All allies reroll all **1s** on all damage dice and must use the new result. This applies to all sources of damage, including attacks, spells, and abilities. |
| **Dirge of Withering** | All enemies suffer **-1 to hit** with all attacks. There is a **1% chance per point of your Luck modifier** that this penalty becomes **-2 to hit**. |
| **Ballad of Ruin** | All enemies take **-1 to all saving throws**. There is a **1% chance per point of your Luck modifier** that this penalty becomes **-2 to all saving throws**. |
| **Refrain of Mending** | At the start of your turn, all allies heal **1d4 HP** and cannot exceed their maximum hit points. |
| **Purging Chorus** | At the start of your turn, all allies have a **5% + 1% chance per point of your Luck modifier per condition** to remove each negative condition affecting them. Each condition is rolled separately. |
| **Echoes of Fury** | All allies have a **5% + 1% chance per point of your Luck modifier** to make an additional attack when they take the **Attack** action. |

## Notes

### Current Role
The Troubadour is currently defined as:
- rear-rank half caster
- support and party-enhancement specialist
- persistent song buff/debuff user
- social and morale-focused utility class
- Luck-scaling momentum support character

### Current Open Mechanical Questions
These may need later clarification:
- whether starting a new Song immediately ends any currently active Song
- whether a Song use is spent only when a Song begins or also during continued maintenance
- whether the upgraded bonus or penalty rolls on Songs are checked once when the Song begins or reevaluated while it persists
- how **Echoes of Fury** behaves in multi-attack sequences and similar edge cases
- whether any future effects can allow the Troubadour to benefit from their own Songs as an exception
