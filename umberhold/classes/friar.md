# Friar

## Class Summary

The **Friar** is a full divine caster who crushes the unclean with relic-bearing zeal, sustains the party with potent healing, and turns the dead through the authority of Solace.

### Core Identity
- **Primary Attributes:** Piety
- **Level 10 Title:** Saint of the Last Rite
- **Hit Die:** d8
- **Armor:** Any armor
- **Shield:** Can equip shield
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** All bludgeoning weapons
- **Spellcasting:** Full caster using **Piety**
- **Special Role:** Full divine caster, undead controller, healer, quarterstaff battle-priest

## Features

### Spellcasting
You can cast spells as a **full caster**.

Rules:
- the Friar uses **Piety** as its spellcasting attribute
- spell access and spell recovery follow Umberhold's current spellcasting rules
- at character creation, a Friar gains all currently approved **F cantrips** and **F 1st-level spells**

---

### Quarterstaff Mastery
Your preferred weapon is the **quarterstaff**.

While wielding a quarterstaff:
- your attacks score a **critical hit on 19-20**
- on a critical hit, the target is **Stunned** until the end of its next turn

---

### Turn Undead
Requires a **holy symbol of Solace**.

As an action, all undead must make a **Piety saving throw** against your **spell DC**.

On a failure:
- they gain the **Frightened** condition
- they may repeat the save at the end of each of their turns

Rules:
- this ability may be used **once per combat**
- it may be used up to **three times before requiring a Rest**
- if an undead creature rolls a **natural 1** on this saving throw, it is **destroyed**

---

### Uncapped Attribute Growth
You can increase:
- one attribute by **2**
- or two attributes by **1** each

---

### Sacred Retribution
When an **undead creature** hits you with a **melee attack**, you may immediately make a melee attack against that creature, provided:
- it is within your reach
- you are wielding a melee weapon

Rules:
- this effect can occur only **once per turn**

---

### Thunderstrike
When you hit with a melee attack, there is a chance that Solace's wrath calls down a bolt of lightning upon your target.

On a successful trigger:
- the target takes an additional **4d6 lightning** damage

### Formula
```text
15% + 1% per point of your Luck modifier
```

---

### Empowered Healing
Your healing spells restore **one additional healing die**, matching the spell's normal healing die.

---

### Turn Undead (Flee)
Undead that fail their saving throw now **flee**.

Rules:
- your party gains **full experience** as if they were slain
- they drop **no treasure**

---

### Sanctified Staff
Your **quarterstaff** attacks deal **one additional die of holy damage**.

---

### Divine Restoration
Your healing spells can now **critically heal**.

When you cast a healing spell, roll a **d20**.
On a result of **20**:
- the healing is **doubled** as per a critical hit

---

### Embrace of Solace
Damage that would reduce you to **0 hit points** instead reduces you to **1 hit point**.

Rules:
- this effect may occur **once**
- it recharges after a **Rest**

---

### Turn Undead (Annihilation)
Undead that fail their saving throw are **destroyed**.

---

## Notes

### Current Role
The Friar is currently defined as:
- full divine caster
- primary healer
- anti-undead controller and destroyer
- quarterstaff-focused battle-priest
- resilient holy support class

### Current Spell Access
Under current Umberhold spellcasting canon, the Friar begins with:
- all approved **F cantrips**
- all approved **F 1st-level spells**

The current early spell list includes:
- **Deathknell of Solace**
- **Cure Minor Wounds**
- **Judgment of Solace**
- **Litany of Solace**
- **Shield of Solace**
- **Solace's Blessing**

### Current Open Mechanical Questions
These may need later clarification:
- how **Turn Undead** resolves against mixed enemy formations and whether all undead in both ranks are always affected
- whether **Turn Undead (Flee)** and **Turn Undead (Annihilation)** fully replace the base frightened result or add to it conditionally
- how **Sacred Retribution** is resolved in Umberhold's no-reaction combat model beyond its explicit once-per-turn trigger
- whether **Empowered Healing** applies to every healing spell uniformly, including rank-wide healing such as **Litany of Solace**
- whether **Divine Restoration** doubles only the final healing total or doubles all healing dice before modifiers
