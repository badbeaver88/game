# Shadowman

## Class Summary

The **Shadowman** is a swift killer, skulking scout, and trap-breaker who thrives on speed, precision, and dirty fighting.

### Core Identity
- **Primary Attributes:** Agility / Luck
- **Level 10 Title:** Dread-Shade
- **Hit Die:** d8
- **Armor:** Light armor
- **Shield:** Cannot equip shield
- **Weapon Proficiencies:** All single-handed melee weapons, shortbow, hand crossbow
- **Special Role:** Lockpicker, trap specialist, stealth striker, poison harvester

## Features

### Slip into Shadows
You vanish into shadow and reappear where the enemy least expects.

If you choose this option on your turn in combat:
- make a **Stealth check** against the highest enemy passive perception
- on a success, you may attack **any enemy in any rank**
- that attack is made with **advantage**
- if the attack hits, you deal extra **Low Blow** damage
- after the attack, you return to your normal position in the party ranks

If you fail to enter stealth:
- you lose the attack
- you suffer no additional penalty

---

### Vigilance
You gain **+4 to initiative rolls**.

---

### Low Blow
You deal extra damage to targets that do not see you coming, usually through **Slip into Shadows**.

### Low Blow Progression
- Level 1: **1d6**
- Level 5: **2d6**
- Level 10: **3d6**

---

### Open Locks
Only a Shadowman has this ability under the current class rules.

A lockpicking check uses:
- **Agility modifier**
- **Proficiency Bonus**
- any relevant tool bonus

Requirements:
- thieves' tools

Rules:
- each lock may be attempted only once per character unless another rule overrides this
- a failed attempt means the lock must be bypassed by other means

---

### Find and Remove Traps
Only a Shadowman has this ability under the current class rules.

Requirements:
- thieves' tools

Rules:
- detecting a trap uses a perception-style check plus Proficiency Bonus
- disabling a trap uses an Agility-based check plus Proficiency Bonus

---

### Anatomical Study
Your attacks now score a critical hit on a **19-20**.

---

### Uncapped Attribute Growth
You can increase:
- one attribute by **2**
- or two attributes by **1** each

---

### Dual Wield
You may use a melee weapon in your off-hand as long as it is **lighter** than your main-hand weapon.

When you take the **Attack** action with your main-hand weapon, you may make:
- **one additional off-hand attack** as a **bonus action**

---

### Riposte
When dual wielding, your **AC increases by 1**.

---

### Venom Harvester
You may make a **DC 15 Survival check** to harvest poison from a poisonous creature.

Rules:
- no special tools are required
- you are assumed to carry small phials for storage
- on a success, you harvest **1d2 doses**
- as an action, you may apply one dose to a melee weapon
- the poison is **single-use** and is expended after the first successful hit
- if you fail the harvesting check by **5 or more**, you are affected by the poison

---

### Dirty Fighting
When you hit, the target takes one additional die of damage—matching the damage die you rolled—at the start of the enemy's next turn as **bleeding** damage.

---

### Evasion
If you succeed on an **Agility saving throw** against an effect that would normally deal half damage on a successful save, you instead take **no damage**.

---

### Master of Shadows
You now have **advantage on all Stealth checks**.

---

### Master Harvester
On a successful Survival check to harvest poison from a poisonous creature:
- you now harvest **1d4 doses**
- you can never poison yourself while harvesting a poisonous creature

---

## Notes

### Current Role
The Shadowman is currently defined as:
- fast melee striker
- stealth infiltrator
- lock and trap specialist
- poison utility class

### Current Open Mechanical Questions
These may need later clarification:
- whether any future items or classes can partially overlap with Shadowman lock and trap expertise
- how poison application interacts with dual-wielding across multiple weapons
- whether Dirty Fighting can trigger from every successful hit in a multi-attack sequence
- whether Slip into Shadows can be used every turn without restriction or should have encounter-based limits later
