# Umberhold Light and Vision Canon

This document captures the current approved rules for:
- first-person seeing distance
- darkness
- fixed dungeon lighting
- portable and magical light sources

It is intended to align exploration readability, equipment-based light rules, and timed light durations under one shared model.

---

## 1. Natural Seeing Distance

In first-person exploration, the party's unaided seeing distance depends on whether they are above ground and how much natural light is available.

### Above Ground
- during daylight-facing phases, the party sees up to **3 tiles ahead**
- during nighttime phases, the party sees up to **2 tiles ahead**

For this rule, daylight-facing outdoor phases are:
- **First Light**
- **High Morning**
- **Sun at Zenith**
- **Fading Light**

All other phases are treated as night for above-ground visibility.

### Dungeons and Other Adventuring Locations
Dungeons and other authored adventuring locations do **not** use outdoor daylight rules.

Instead, each location defines whether it is:
- **lit**
- **unlit**

A lit location provides ambient visibility through its authored presentation and does not require a party-held light source.

An unlit location is completely dark unless the party is using an active light source.

---

## 2. Darkness

In a completely dark location:
- the party may still move
- they cannot see ahead
- effective navigation requires a light source

### Collision in Darkness
If the party walks into a solid wall while in darkness:
- display the message **"Ouch."**
- play an audible impact cue

---

## 3. Light Sources

The party may create exploration light with:
- the **Light** spell
- a **Hooded Lantern**
- a **Torch**

Any other spell or item that explicitly grants illumination should follow the same strongest-source rule unless it says otherwise.

### Equip Requirement
A handheld light source must be equipped in either:
- **main hand**
- **off-hand**

to provide illumination.

### Stacking Rule
If multiple light sources are active at the same time:
- only the **strongest** illumination range applies

### Override Rule
An active light source overrides:
- dungeon darkness
- outdoor night darkness

and provides its full illumination range.

### Hard Cutoff
Beyond the current active light or visibility range, there is **complete darkness**.

---

## 4. Light Spell

The approved **Light** spell functions as a party light source in first-person exploration.

### Rules
- provides **3 tiles of illumination**
- follows the general strongest-source and darkness-override rules
- spell duration continues to be governed by the spell list entry

---

## 5. Hooded Lantern

A hooded lantern burns for **4 hours** on **one flask of oil**.

Under the shared time system, this equals **2,400 time steps**.

### Fuel Use
When a lantern is lit:
- oil is first taken automatically from the carrying character's inventory
- if the carrier has no oil, one flask is taken from a **random other party member**
- if the party has no oil, display:

> **You have no oil to fill the lantern.**

### Use Rules
- the lantern must be equipped in **main hand** or **off-hand**
- it provides **3 tiles of illumination**
- beyond that range there is complete darkness

### Expiration
When the lantern expires, display:

> **Your lantern has gone out.**

---

## 6. Torch

A torch burns for **1 hour**.

Under the shared time system, this equals **600 time steps**.

### Use Rules
- the torch must be equipped in **main hand** or **off-hand**
- it provides **3 tiles of illumination**
- beyond that range there is complete darkness
- its light should show a subtle flicker of shifting shadows within the lit radius

### Expiration
When the torch expires, display:

> **Your torch has gone out.**

---

## 7. Status

This category is **approved canon** for the current design direction.
